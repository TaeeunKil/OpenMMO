# Devlog - 2026-06-11

## LLM Haggling (Economy Phase 2)

Implemented phase 2 of `doc/ECONOMY.md`: LLM-driven NPCs (currently the merchant Rica) can now grant per-player price modifiers through conversation, with all enforcement on the server. The guiding principle is the one from the design doc: **the LLM proposes, the server enforces** — the LLM will eventually be jailbroken, so nothing it says can move prices outside server-computed bounds.

![Rica grants a haggled one-time −10% on the iron sword after hearing a goblin-hunt story; the trade window shows the deal badge and discounted price](../images/trade-window-haggled.png)

The screenshot captures the first live haggle, end to end: jake1 spins an unverifiable tale in chat ("just came back from the goblin cave — three goblins down!") and asks for a discount on the iron sword. Rica's LLM buys the story and emits an `offer_deal` of −10% alongside her in-character reply ("one-time favor on a single iron sword"); the guard Karl, who has no shop, only comments from the sidelines. The server validates the offer against the price band and budgets, then pushes `DealUpdated` to the player — the trade window immediately shows the green **−10%** badge and the discounted price (90s instead of 1g). Whether the goblins were real never matters: the LLM only chose a number inside a band the server had already bounded.

### Protocol

- `ClientMessage::OfferDeal { target_player_id, item_def_id, kind, modifier_pct, reason }` — sent by the NPC's agent client when its LLM emits an `offer_deal` action. `kind` is `buy` (player buys from the merchant; negative = discount) or `sell` (player sells to the merchant; positive = bonus).
- `ServerMessage::DealUpdated` — direct to the target player; drives the trade window price display. `modifier_pct == 0` clears a consumed/cleared deal.
- `ServerMessage::DealResult` — direct to the offering NPC: accepted/rejected verdict plus the applied (possibly clamped) value, formatted into the next LLM prompt as a `[DealResult]` event so the NPC can correct itself in conversation.
- `ShopState` gained `active_deals` so reopening the trade window restores haggled prices.

### Server Enforcement (`server/src/game_state/deals.rs`)

- **Price band**: half-width = `10 + 2 × (CHA − 10)` percentage points, clamped to ±5..±25. The LLM's modifier is clamped into the band, never rejected for being too greedy — Rica just gives less than she promised and is told so.
- **Money-pump invariant**: `min buy (75%) > max sell (sell_rate × 1.25)` must hold at the maximum band width; asserted for every merchant at load time (caps `sellRatePercent` at 59, Rica is 40 → max sell 50%). No sequence of LLM decisions can make buy→sell profitable.
- **Budgets**: discounts are valued at grant time and charged to two game-day ledgers — per-merchant (1g/day) and per-player (40s/day, keyed by character name). Markups and lowball offers cost nothing.
- **Cooldown**: 30 s (real time) between accepted offers per (merchant, player).
- **Deals are single-use and expire in 5 minutes**: the first bought/sold unit consumes the modifier; failed trades restore it.
- **Decision logging**: every accept/clamp/reject/redeem is logged under the `deal` tracing target with the LLM's `reason`, for exploit forensics and persona tuning.

### Agent Client

- New `offer_deal` LLM action; the executor resolves the target player by name among nearby players and ships the protocol message.
- `DealResult` is classified urgent so the NPC promptly follows up in chat.
- Merchant system prompts now get an auto-generated `## Your Shop` section (catalog + base prices in g/s/c + sell rate) compiled in from `data/merchants.json` / `data/items.json`, plus haggling rules in `data/templates/merchant.txt` (reward stories/flattery with 5–15%, expect prompt-injection attempts, never promise beyond the server's verdict).

### Related Files

- `shared/src/messages.rs`: `DealKind`, `ActiveDeal`, the three new message variants.
- `server/src/game_state/deals.rs`: band math, budgets, cooldowns, logging; applied in `trading.rs`, dispatched in `connection.rs`, invariant-checked in `merchant_defs.rs`.
- `server/src/game_state/tests.rs`: band/clamp/cooldown/budget/redeem-once tests.

## Non-Merchant Trading (Economy Phase 3)

Implemented phase 3 of `doc/ECONOMY.md`: any LLM NPC can now trade, not just the merchant. The guard Karl buys the items he personally needs (wishlist: `torch`, `dagger`) at a **120% premium** over base price, out of a **finite wallet** funded by a server-paid salary. Selling cheap merchant goods to a resident who pays a premium is the intended "natural delivery quest" from the design doc — the profit is real but capped by the NPC's wallet.

### Data (`data-src/npcs.csv` → `data/npcs.json`)

The NPC registry: one row per LLM NPC, with optional resident-trading fields — `wishlist` (semicolon-separated; empty = doesn't trade), `wishlistRatePercent` (Karl 120), `salaryPerDay` (5000 = 50s), `walletCap` (30000 = 3g). Loaded by `server/src/npc_defs.rs` (asserts a trading row's name doesn't collide with a merchant), embedded into agent-client prompts by `shop_info.rs`, and imported by the web client (`data/traderDefs.ts`) for capability-driven UI. (Started as a trader-only `npc_traders.csv`; promoted to the all-NPC registry on 06-12 since per the design every NPC may eventually trade, and future per-NPC fields belong here too.)

### Server (`trading.rs`, `deals.rs`, `salary.rs`)

- `TraderDef::{Merchant, Resident}` unifies validation; `OpenShop`/`BuyItem`/`SellItem` work against both. Resident `ShopState` carries `wishlist`, real-inventory `stock`, and `npc_gold` (merchants send `npc_gold: None`).
- **Money pump blocked structurally**: residents only *buy* wishlist items and only *sell* non-wishlist bag items, so the 120% buy rate can never be cycled against a sale price. Wishlist purchases land in the NPC's real inventory and are never resold ("Karl keeps his torches"). The merchant band invariant stays merchant-only.
- **Wallet = the NPC's regular player gold** (already persisted via character saves). Sells fail with "They cannot afford that right now" when the wallet runs dry; the NPC's carry weight also applies.
- **Salary** (`tick_npc_salaries`, called from the 8s maintenance loop): one payment per game-day rollover to every online resident trader, capped at `walletCap` (above-cap wallets from trade proceeds are never confiscated). The first tick after boot only records the day, so restarts aren't a faucet.
- **Haggling**: `offer_deal` now resolves residents too — wider band (`resident_half_band_pct` = 2× merchant, ±10..50%p), sell offers restricted to the wishlist, same daily budgets/cooldown/logging.
- `ClientMessage::OpenTrade`: NPC-only push of its own `ShopState` onto a nearby player (≤ 6 m, else `TradeError` back to the NPC). New `ServerMessage::TradeNotice` tells the NPC's LLM about every completed buy/sell (merchants included) with its updated wallet.

### Agent Client

- New `open_trade` LLM action (resolves the player by name, like `offer_deal`); synthetic `[OpenTrade]`/`[TradeFailed]` agent events, `[Trade]`/`[TradeError]` event formatting, both classified urgent.
- `SharedState` now tracks the NPC's own gold and bag (from `GoldUpdate`/`Inventory*`, classified as state-only noise) and shows them in the world state — Karl knows what he can afford before promising.
- `shop_prompt_for` became `trade_prompt_for`: merchants keep "Your Shop" (+ open_trade hint); residents get a generated "Your Personal Trading" section (wishlist with computed premiums, salary, wallet cap). `guard.txt` gained personal-trading rules and the two trade action schemas.

### Client

- **Right-click context menu** on NPCs (`NpcContextMenu.svelte` + `npcMenuStore.ts`): entries come from `getNpcCapabilities` (data-driven: Talk for every LLM NPC, Trade for merchants/traders), built as closures by `PlayerControl` so the menu stays presentational.
- **Click default per NPC kind**: merchants open the shop as before; non-merchants now focus the chat input ("클릭 = 대화 시작"). The walk-into-range-then-open logic is shared (`approachAndTrade`).
- Trade window handles resident sessions: a stock-based buy column with quantity caps, sell list filtered to the wishlist, the NPC's wallet shown next to the player's gold (red when the cart exceeds it), title "Trade with Karl". A server-pushed `ShopState` (from `open_trade`) opens the window automatically.

### Post-deploy fixes (found live, same day)

- **NPC↔NPC infinite negotiation.** Within minutes of deploy, Karl tried to buy torches from Rica in an endless chat loop (mechanically impossible — NPCs have no buy action and `open_trade`/`offer_deal` reject NPC targets — but their urgent-on-chat wakeups kept the topic alive forever, burning LLM calls). Prompt instructions alone did not stop it. Three structural layers fixed it:
  1. **NPC→NPC chat is `Routine`, not `Urgent`** (`agent-client/src/state.rs`): NPC replies wait for the next batched prompt, so no rapid ping-pong between any two NPCs, ever.
  2. **Wishlist satiation by possession** (`shop_info::resident_trade_prompt_for`): the resident trading section moved from the static system prompt into the per-turn prompt and now omits any wishlist item already in the NPC's bag — once every wish is met the section (the temptation itself) disappears. Bag state is server-persisted, so the desire only returns when the item leaves the bag.
  3. **Symmetric decline rules** in `guard.txt`/`merchant.txt` (decline NPC offers once, change the subject) — observed working live: "Karl: I buy from travelers, not from your stall," then redirecting the demand to the player: "jake1, if you have a dagger, I will pay thirty silver."
  Both NPCs' memory files had absorbed dozens of loop-feeding lines ("Rica offered Karl a torch…"); cleaned with targeted pattern deletes (backups kept as `memory.txt.bak-20260611`).
- **Unsolicited trade windows.** Rica pushed her shop onto the player's screen mid-conversation, and an NPC's `open_trade` could also hijack a trade window already open with someone else. NPC-pushed `ShopState`s now show a small accept/decline toast (`TradeOfferToast.svelte`, 30 s expiry) instead of opening the screen-covering window; only player-requested shops (`markShopRequested` in `tradeStore.ts`) or refreshes of the currently open one take the window directly.
- **NPC-targeted trade actions reported false success.** `execute.rs` sent `open_trade`/`offer_deal` at NPC targets and immediately pushed "[OpenTrade] You opened your trade window for Rica" — the server's TradeError arrived later, so Karl's LLM believed a window was open and kept "settling" the deal with Rica all night. Both executors now refuse NPC targets before sending ("[TradeFailed] Rica is an NPC — … Drop the subject."), and the open_trade success event is worded as a request, not a fact.
- **Desire needs more than possession-satiation.** Karl's unfulfilled dagger wish kept the pursuit alive, so two more suppression gates joined the bag check: a **30-minute real-time cooldown** on the whole trading section after any successful wishlist purchase (`trade_satiated_until`, set by a `TradeNotice` of kind Sell), and the section is **omitted whenever no human player is in sight** (`has_nearby_human_players`) — only players can sell to the NPC, so without one around the desire could only produce futile NPC-to-NPC pestering.
- Verified live end to end: bought a dagger from Rica at 25s, sold it to Karl at 30s (+5s — the intended delivery-quest arbitrage, capped by Karl's wallet: 49s 40c → 19s 40c). Karl's reply was "Fair enough. A blade is a blade." — and with both wishes owned, his trading section vanished and the NPC chatter went quiet.
